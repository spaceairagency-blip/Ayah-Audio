Section 01
Information We Collect

Quran Player is designed with your privacy as a priority. We collect only the minimal information necessary to provide a functional, personalized audio experience. We do not require account registration, and we do not collect personally identifiable information such as your name, email address, or phone number.

The following categories of information may be processed:

  • Playback preferences — your selected reciter, volume, skip duration, and display settings are stored locally on your device.
  • Surah and ayah position — to restore your listening position between sessions, stored entirely on your device.
  • Device connectivity status — checked locally to display the offline notice. No data is sent to our servers.
  • Anonymous usage patterns — only if explicitly enabled through an analytics service, and fully anonymized with no link to your identity.
Section 02
How We Use Information

Any information stored is used solely to deliver and improve your experience within the Quran Player application. Specifically, we use data to:

  1. Remember your reciter selection, volume level, and display preferences across sessions.
  2. Resume playback from your last listened surah and ayah.
  3. Apply your skip-duration and sleep-timer settings instantly.
  4. Display the appropriate Arabic subtitle and Bangla translation panels based on your toggle choices.
  5. Detect offline status locally so we can notify you clearly without streaming attempts failing silently.
We do not sell, rent, trade, or otherwise share your preferences or usage data with any third party for commercial purposes. Your listening habits are your own.
Section 03
Third-Party Services

To deliver Quranic audio and translations, the Quran Player communicates with the following external services. Each has its own privacy policy which we encourage you to review:

  • AlQuran.cloud API — provides Arabic text, Bangla (Bengali) translations, and per-ayah audio streams for all 114 surahs across multiple reciters. Your IP address may be visible to this service when audio is streamed.
  • Google Fonts — serves the Amiri, Cinzel Decorative, and Crimson Pro typefaces used in the interface. Google may log font requests per their standard CDN policy.
  • Font Awesome CDN — serves icon assets. Standard CDN logging may apply.

We have no control over how these third parties process data. We choose providers whose services are trusted, widely used, and appropriate for a Quranic application. If you are on a restricted network or wish to avoid third-party CDN requests, please consult each provider's privacy documentation.

Section 04
Local Storage & Cookies

The Quran Player uses the browser's localStorage API to persist your settings between visits. This is a client-side mechanism — data is stored only on your device and is never transmitted to our servers.

The following key is used in localStorage:

  • quranPlayerSettings2 — stores your reciter choice, volume, skip durations, shuffle/repeat state, and visibility toggles for Arabic and Bangla panels.

We do not set any tracking cookies. We do not use advertising cookies, analytics cookies, or fingerprinting techniques. You may clear localStorage at any time through your browser's settings, which will reset all player preferences to their defaults.

Section 05
Audio Streaming

All Quranic audio is streamed on-demand from the AlQuran.cloud API. Audio is not hosted on our own servers, and we do not cache or store audio on our infrastructure. Recitation audio files are served directly from the API provider to your device.

When you select a surah and press play, your device makes a direct HTTP request to api.alquran.cloud. Your IP address is visible to that service as part of the standard internet request process — this is the same as visiting any website.

Translation data (both Arabic text and Bangla translation) fetched from the same API is cached temporarily in memory during your session to reduce repeated network calls. This in-session cache is cleared when you close or refresh the app.

Section 06
Children's Privacy

The Quran Player is a spiritual and educational tool suitable for all ages, including children. We have designed it specifically to collect no personal information whatsoever — there are no accounts, no sign-ups, and no forms requiring any user data.

Because we collect no personal data from any user, the application is inherently compliant with children's privacy considerations. Parents and guardians can use this application with full confidence that no information about their children is being collected or shared.

If you have any concerns about your child's use of this application, please contact us at the address provided in the Contact section below.

Section 07
Data Security

Because we do not collect or store personal data on our servers, our attack surface is minimal by design. The settings data stored in your browser's localStorage is protected by your device's own security model — it is accessible only to the origin domain of the Quran Player application.

We employ the following practices to ensure a secure experience:

  • All API communications are made over HTTPS, ensuring that requests between your device and AlQuran.cloud are encrypted in transit.
  • No user credentials are ever required or requested.
  • No third-party advertising scripts, tracking pixels, or analytics SDKs are embedded in the application code.
  • The application does not request microphone, camera, or location permissions.
Section 08
Your Rights

As a user of the Quran Player, you retain full control over any data stored on your device. Since all preference data is stored locally via localStorage, you may:

  • Access your data — open your browser's developer tools, navigate to Application → Local Storage, and inspect the quranPlayerSettings2 key.
  • Delete your data — clear your browser's site data or localStorage for this domain at any time to remove all saved preferences.
  • Opt out of third-party CDNs — use a browser extension such as uBlock Origin to block requests to Google Fonts or Font Awesome if you wish. The app will fall back to system fonts.
  • Request information — contact us at any time if you have questions about what data may exist relating to your usage.

Users in the European Union (GDPR), United Kingdom (UK GDPR), California (CCPA), and other privacy-law jurisdictions are entitled to the rights described above. Since we do not process personal data, most data subject request rights are satisfied by design.
